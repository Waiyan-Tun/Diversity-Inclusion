# Diversity & Inclusion Dashboard

## Data Analysis & Visualization Project

### Introduction

This Diversity & Inclusion (D&I) dashboard is developed to analyze workforce diversity, gender distribution, performance ratings, promotions, turnover, and hiring patterns within an organization.

The goal of this project is to practice and apply the **Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyze, Share, Act** framework to uncover insights that support data-driven HR and organizational decisions.

---

## Background of Study

The dataset contains employee information such as gender, job level, department, performance rating, age group, promotion history, hires, and turnover data.

## 1. Ask

### Business Task

Identify workforce diversity patterns and assess whether gender and age groups are represented fairly across performance ratings, promotions, leadership positions, and turnover.

### Key Questions

* What is the gender balance across all job levels and executive roles?
* Are promotions distributed fairly between men and women?
* How does performance rating vary by gender or age group?
* Do turnover rates suggest potential equality gaps?
* What hiring patterns exist across job levels and genders?

## 4. Analyze

This section summarizes key findings from both **Page 1** (Workforce Overview) and **Page 2** (Hiring, Promotion, and Turnover).

###  **Page 1: Hiring, Promotion & Turnover**

#### **Hiring by Gender**

* **41% of hires were women**, **59% were men**.
* Women were hired more frequently in junior roles than leadership roles.

  * Example:

    * Junior Officer: **53% female hires**
    * Executive: **13% female hires**

#### **Promotions by Gender**

* Women represented:

  * **15.8% of promoted Executives**
  * **19.3% of promoted Senior Managers**
  * **31.6% of promoted Managers**
  * **52.1% of promoted Senior Officers**

Women receive more promotions in lower job levels but significantly fewer in senior roles.

#### **Turnover Rate (FY20 Leavers)**

* **Female turnover:** 11%
* **Male turnover:** 9%

Performance rating comparison between leavers and non-leavers shows:

* Leavers (both genders) tend to have **lower performance scores** than employees who stayed.
* Turnover is higher among mid-level job grades.

## 6. Act

### Recommendations for Diversity & Inclusion Improvement

1. **Increase Female Representation in Leadership**

   * Implement leadership development programs targeting high-performing women.

2. **Review Promotion Policies for Senior Roles**

   * Ensure promotion criteria are transparent and gender-neutral.

3. **Targeted Hiring Strategies**

   * Improve gender diversity in hiring for Director and Executive roles.

4. **Address Turnover Among Women**

   * Conduct exit interviews focused on career progression and workplace experience.

5. **Mentorship Programs**

   * Pair junior female employees with senior leaders to support advancement.

6. **Bias Awareness and Inclusive Culture Training**

   * Train managers on recognition, evaluation, and promotion bias.
